# Executive Overview — Next Vision MVP

## The Strategic Question

**Foundation MVP delivers:** Governed, rules-driven narrative with AI as constrained executor.

**Next Vision answers:** What comes after technical excellence?

**Answer:** Features that create emotional depth, emergent content, and experiences impossible in traditional media.

---

## The Five Transformative Features

### 1. Cross-Session Memory — Characters That Remember

**Problem:** Every playthrough starts fresh. Characters forget you.  
**Solution:** Persistent relationship development across sessions.  
**Impact:** Players form real attachments. "She remembers me."

**Key Insight:** Memory creates the illusion of consciousness.

---

### 2. Procedural Subplots — Stories That Generate Themselves

**Problem:** Authored content ends. Replay = same content.  
**Solution:** Narrative DNA generates coherent subplots from templates.  
**Impact:** Infinite replayability. Every playthrough unique.

**Key Insight:** Structure enables emergence without chaos.

---

### 3. Multi-Perspective Engine — See Through Different Eyes

**Problem:** Single POV limits emotional understanding.  
**Solution:** Switch perspectives mid-scene. Experience dramatic irony.  
**Impact:** Unprecedented narrative depth and moral complexity.

**Key Insight:** Multiple truths create richer stories than single truth.

---

### 4. Multiplayer Narrative — Shared Drama with Friends

**Problem:** Interactive drama is lonely.  
**Solution:** 2-4 players control different characters in same story.  
**Impact:** Social experience. Emergent player dynamics.

**Key Insight:** Drama is best experienced together.

---

### 5. Meta-Narrative Layer — Stories That Know They're Stories

**Problem:** Narrative constraints feel invisible but limiting.  
**Solution:** Characters aware of story structure. Can resist, comment, negotiate.  
**Impact:** Philosophical depth. Nothing else like it exists.

**Key Insight:** Self-awareness creates new forms of storytelling.

## Technical Feasibility

### What We Know Works
- LLMs can maintain consistency across context (cross-session memory)
- Template-based generation can be coherent (procedural subplots)
- Multiple model calls can represent different POVs (multi-perspective)
- Turn-based systems support multiplayer (collaborative narrative)
- Meta-commentary is technically trivial (meta-layer)

### What's Unproven
- **Quality at scale:** Will procedural subplots feel authored?
- **Player adoption:** Do people WANT multi-POV switching?
- **Technical complexity:** Can we handle 4 concurrent player perspectives?
- **Meta reception:** Is fourth-wall breaking immersive or jarring?

### Risk Level by Feature
- Cross-Session Memory: **Low risk** (proven mechanics)
- Procedural Subplots: **Medium risk** (quality control challenge)
- Multi-Perspective: **Medium risk** (UX complexity)
- Multiplayer: **High risk** (coordination, networking)
- Meta-Layer: **Very high risk** (polarizing, experimental)

---

## Implementation Philosophy

**NOT:** Build everything at once

**Phase 1:** Cross-Session Memory (proof of emotional impact)  
**Phase 2:** Procedural Subplots (proof of quality generation)  
**Phase 3:** Multi-Perspective (proof of UX viability)  
**Phase 4:** Multiplayer (proof of social dynamics)  
**Phase 5:** Meta-Layer (proof of concept, experimental)

**Each phase de-risks the next.**
