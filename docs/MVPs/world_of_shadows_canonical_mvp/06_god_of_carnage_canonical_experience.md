# God of Carnage canonical experience

## Slice identity

God of Carnage is the first canonical World of Shadows vertical slice.
It is the current proof-bearing center of gravity for the MVP.

The slice is:

- a guided interactive drama,
- built on structured authored content,
- entered through free-language player input,
- shaped by scene-aware runtime semantics,
- and governed by truth-aligned validation and commit.

It is not a general-purpose chat surface.

## What the slice must prove

The slice is successful only if it demonstrates:

- source-aware dramatic steering,
- stable character identity,
- explicit scene direction,
- truth-aligned visible output,
- consequence continuity,
- credible live interaction,
- and a runtime structure that can later be exposed safely through control-plane tooling without surrendering truth authority.

## Source-to-game transformation

The slice is not supposed to merely preserve source text.
It must transform source into playable dramatic machinery, including:

- scene cores,
- pressure ladders,
- move families,
- relationship fault lines,
- reveal surfaces,
- escalation and de-escalation routes,
- repair possibilities,
- continuity hooks,
- and direction material strong enough to drive play.

## Character minds and scene direction

The slice preserves two central responsibilities that must not collapse into generic response generation:

### Character minds
Important figures must remain stable in:

- tactic,
- pressure response,
- relationship posture,
- motive protection,
- and performance signature.

### Scene direction
The runtime must not collapse into disconnected local turns or summary-heavy prose.
Scene direction remains first-class and includes:

- responder choice,
- scene function,
- pacing mode,
- brevity and silence handling,
- and fallback shaping that still feels scene-led.

## Canonical dramatic turn target

The slice contracts preserve the following target phases:

1. player move interpretation,
2. scene assessment,
3. responder selection,
4. scene-function selection,
5. pacing / silence / brevity shaping,
6. proposed state effects,
7. validation outcome,
8. committed result,
9. visible output shaping,
10. continuity update,
11. diagnostics and provenance capture.

The point of this structure is not bureaucracy.
The point is to keep dramatic richness compatible with truth discipline.

## Canonical visible behavior restored from the A-F closure line

### A-line — the reply belongs to the right live pressure
The player-facing line must make these things legible when active:

- who is effectively answering,
- side or pair geometry,
- exchange type,
- carryover wound,
- and room / object / hosting-surface pressure.

That includes room-bound live surfaces such as doorway pressure, bathroom-edge exposure, books, flowers, the phone, and hosting tension when they are the hottest current social surface.

### B-line — characters must not sound interchangeable
The slice already supports stronger differentiation than name labels alone.
The source corpus repeatedly emphasizes distinct performance signatures, including:

- Véronique's principle-first rebuke,
- Annette's cutting contradiction,
- Michel's smoothing deflection,
- Alain's tired evasive hedge.

### C-line — replies must remember more than the immediately previous line
Two continuity behaviors are now part of the canonical experience reading:

- immediate prior-answer continuity,
- and delayed reuse of an earlier answer after one intervening turn when the same pressure surface returns.

### D-line — interruption, dodge, hesitation, and re-entry matter
The slice now canonically includes:

- interruption pressure,
- deflection and forced re-entry,
- unfinished social moves that remain active,
- and hesitation behavior where dramatically appropriate.

### E-line — exchange choreography matters
The current slice truth also includes:

- tighter social handoff,
- faster dramatic threading,
- and less flat single-speaker reset behavior.

### F-line — pressure can relay across multiple speakers
The strongest restored experience behavior includes:

- same-surface pressure picked up by another speaker,
- multi-speaker relay before a dodge settles the exchange,
- and a more theatrical room-feel than a simple alternating Q/A loop.

## Truth-aligned visible output rules

No matter how rich the dramatic behavior becomes, these rules stay fixed:

- nothing visible without truth support,
- nothing committed and dramatically relevant stays wholly invisible,
- partial rejection must not create stronger visible consequence than commit allows,
- and ambiguity may exist, but not as a cover for missing runtime commitment.

## Canonical reading

God of Carnage is the current slice where World of Shadows most strongly proves that a **truth-bound dramatic runtime** can feel socially alive, room-aware, and character-specific without becoming ungoverned improvisation.
