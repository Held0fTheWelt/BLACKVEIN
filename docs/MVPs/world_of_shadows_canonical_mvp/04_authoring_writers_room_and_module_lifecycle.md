# Authoring, writers' room, and module lifecycle

## Canonical position

World of Shadows was never only a play surface.
Across the complete MVPs, the v21 restoration package, and the v23-v24 repository docs, it is also an **authoring, review, and governed revision system**.

That broader product truth must remain canonical even though the current strongest proof is concentrated around one active slice.

## Structured authored modules remain the source of dramatic possibility space

The system assumes structured authored content rather than unbounded runtime invention.
At minimum, modules define or derive:

- characters,
- relationships,
- scenes,
- scene transitions,
- triggers,
- escalation axes,
- end states,
- direction material,
- and continuity hooks strong enough to drive play rather than commentary.

For the active slice, the GoC module under `content/modules/god_of_carnage/` is the concrete realization of this principle.

## Writers' room intelligence remains canonical target truth

The writers' room platform from the complete MVPs preserved a real capability family, not decorative future fluff.
It includes:

- character definition studio,
- arc designer,
- foreshadowing planner,
- motif and symbol planning,
- scene QA,
- continuity inspector,
- version-aware story review,
- and surfaces for spotting drift, missing callbacks, overloaded reveal windows, and pacing flatlines.

The later repository adds a different but compatible framing around writers' room governance and overlap boundaries with retrieval.
Together they establish a clear canonical direction: authoring is governed, reviewable, and diagnostically supported.

## Validation before publish

The preserved authoring truth includes validation before publish, such as:

- character-voice consistency,
- arc pacing,
- foreshadowing payoff coverage,
- relationship logic,
- thematic drift detection,
- and continuity review.

This aligns with the later publish/review/governance posture rather than contradicting it.

## Authoring analytics, QA, and live tuning

The v13 line explicitly added capabilities that still matter as canonical target truth:

- story-health dashboard,
- player behavior analytics,
- replay and counterfactual tools,
- live tuning / god-mode under governance,
- A/B testing,
- narrative version control,
- automated story QA,
- and cost / performance profiling.

These should not be restated as current full implementation claims, but they are materially relevant system intent and belong in the canonical MVP.

## Gold data and evaluation operations

The historical MVPs also preserved a more disciplined evaluation posture than simple score formulas.
The canonical target includes:

- gold-data sources,
- labeling guides,
- reviewer roles,
- tie-break policy,
- refresh cadence,
- conflict adjudication,
- and evaluation grounded in actual annotation practice.

That family of requirements remains part of the system's proof-bearing character.

## Research, revision, and canon improvement

The later v21-v24 repo adds stronger surfaces for:

- research intake,
- canon improvement,
- revision review,
- narrative governance,
- operational settings governance,
- and AI-assisted but governed improvement loops.

Those surfaces are not separate from the historical writers' room idea.
They are the newer, more repository-adjacent continuation of it.

## Honest status reading

The correct consolidated reading is:

- structured authoring and pre-publish review are canonical,
- writers' room intelligence and authoring analytics remain canonical target truth,
- parts of this family are present in docs, governance, and adjacent repo surfaces,
- but the active repository is still more proven as a runtime slice than as a fully operational authoring platform.
