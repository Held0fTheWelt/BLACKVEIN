# Final consolidation extension report

## Purpose

This report records the final focused extension added after the broader re-audit package.

The goal was not to broaden the World of Shadows MVP again.
The goal was to harden the canonical MVP against the most likely future loss patterns while keeping the focus on the existing proof-bearing center.

## What was added

Three new canonical MVP documents were added:

1. `11_claim_status_and_proof_map.md`
2. `12_god_of_carnage_acceptance_and_experience_proof_matrix.md`
3. `13_authoring_retrieval_and_runtime_boundary_contract.md`

## Why these additions were justified

These additions were supported by the re-audit findings and the improvement review:

- the canonical set still needed sharper claim-versus-proof visibility,
- GoC needed a more explicit acceptance layer so its proven dramatic seams would not collapse back into vague summary wording,
- and writers' room / retrieval / runtime overlap needed a compact boundary contract so future cleanups would not flatten a governed tension into a hidden second-truth risk.

## Why this does not lose focus

The extension does not add new feature families.
It only makes existing, already-supported MVP truth more legible and harder to lose.

No broader target layer was promoted to "fully live" by wording alone.
No residue was reclassified as closed without new proof.

## Result

The final package is still a focused canonical MVP package centered on the active God of Carnage slice and the World of Shadows runtime law family.

It is now safer against:
- future documentation compression,
- accidental overclaiming,
- accidental underclaiming,
- and boundary confusion across authoring, retrieval, and runtime.
